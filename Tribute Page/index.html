Highlights of Allen Iverson's NBA Career:

1996 Iverson was drafted with the first overall pick of the NBA draft by the Philadelphia 76ers.
1997 Rookie of the Year after averaging 23.5 points, including a 50-point game against Cleveland.
1999 Selected for All NBA First team and led the NBA in scoring with 26.8 points per game (ppg).
2001 Iverson won his second scoring title and was named league MVP after averaging 31.1 points per game (ppg). He leads the Sixers to the NBA Finals, where they lost to the Los Angeles Lakers.
2002 Leads NBA scoring with 31.4 points per game (ppg), steals with 2.80 per game and minutes played of 42.7 per game.
2004 He was selected to play for USA's Olympic team where they won the bronze medal along side NBA rookies like; Lebron James, Dwane Wade and Carmelo Anthony.
2005 Iverson scores a career-high of 60 points against Orlando.
2006 Traded to Denver Nuggets. At the time of the trade, Iverson was the NBA's number two leading scorer, with new teammate Carmelo Anthony being number one.
2013 Announced his official retirement from basketball, citing he lost his desire to play.
